# Africa Energy Tracker — Scraper AI Classification Training Document

**Version:** 2.0 | **Date:** 28 March 2026
**Based on:** Manual review of 180 Solar-tagged projects that revealed systematic misclassification patterns

## PART 1: THE 12-SECTOR TAXONOMY (Canonical & Final)

The scraper AI must classify every project into exactly ONE of these 12 sectors. No other values are permitted. If a project does not clearly fit any sector, it should be flagged for human review rather than force-classified.

### Sector Definitions with Classification Rules

#### 1. Solar
**Includes:** Solar PV farms, CSP (concentrated solar power) plants, solar thermal systems, solar mini-grids, solar home systems, solar-battery hybrid projects (where solar is the primary generation), floating solar, agri-PV, solar-powered desalination, solar-powered irrigation pumps.

**Classification signals:** "solar farm", "solar park", "photovoltaic", "PV", "CSP", "concentrated solar", "solar home system", "solar mini-grid", "solar IPP", capacity in MWp or MWac.

**Common false positive to AVOID:** Projects that mention "solar" incidentally but are primarily grid infrastructure, battery storage, or mixed-technology programs. If the project name says "Battery" or "Storage" and solar is just the charging source, classify as Battery & Storage.

#### 2. Wind
**Includes:** Onshore wind farms, offshore wind farms, wind parks, wind IPPs.

**Classification signals:** "wind farm", "wind park", "wind power", "wind turbine", "onshore wind", "offshore wind", capacity specified in MW with turbine counts.

#### 3. Hydro
**Includes:** Large hydroelectric dams, run-of-river hydro, small hydro (<10 MW), pumped-storage hydropower (PHS), multipurpose dam projects where hydropower is a component, tidal energy, wave energy.

**Classification signals:** "hydropower", "hydroelectric", "hydro dam", "run-of-river", "pumped storage", "PHS", "multipurpose dam", "tidal", "wave energy". Also: project descriptions mentioning turbine halls, penstocks, reservoirs, spillways.

**Lesson from review:** Two projects were misclassified as Solar that were clearly Hydro:
- "Dogo-Bis Multipurpose Dam Program" (Benin) — the word "Dam" is a dead giveaway
- "Ifahsa Pumped Hydropower Storage" (Morocco) — "Pumped Hydropower" is explicit

#### 4. Geothermal
**Includes:** Geothermal power plants, geothermal wells, geothermal direct-use facilities, geothermal exploration programs tied to a specific site.

**Classification signals:** "geothermal", "steam field", "geothermal well", "Olkaria", "Menengai", "Corbetti", "Aluto Langano". Key countries: Kenya, Ethiopia, Djibouti, Tanzania, Uganda.

#### 5. Oil & Gas
**Includes:** Upstream (exploration, drilling, production), midstream (pipelines, LNG terminals, FLNG, gas processing), downstream (refineries, LPG distribution, fuel depots), gas-to-power plants, natural gas combined cycle plants.

**Classification signals:** "oil", "gas", "petroleum", "refinery", "LNG", "FLNG", "pipeline", "upstream", "downstream", "crude", "barrel", "gas-to-power", "CCGT", "gas turbine", "FPSO".

**Note:** LPG distribution and cooking gas programs go here, NOT under Clean Cooking (which is for improved cookstove and clean fuel programs).

#### 6. Grid Expansion
**Includes:** High-voltage transmission lines, power interconnectors between countries, substations, distribution network upgrades, rural electrification grid extensions, power system strengthening, SCADA/grid management systems.

**Classification signals:** "transmission line", "interconnector", "substation", "distribution", "grid", "power line", "kV line", "HVDC", "power pool", "grid extension", "electrification program" (when focused on grid extension, not generation).

**Lesson from review:** Four projects were misclassified as Solar that were clearly Grid:
- "Zambia Grid Diversification PPA Programme" — "Grid Diversification" is the clue
- "Tanzania-Uganda Interconnector Project" — "Interconnector" = Grid Expansion
- "Huila-Cunene Interconnection Project" — "Interconnection" = Grid Expansion

**CRITICAL distinction:** "Grid-connected solar farm" is Solar (the generation asset). "Grid extension to connect solar farm" is Grid Expansion (the transmission asset). Classify based on what is actually being built/financed.

#### 7. Battery & Storage
**Includes:** Grid-scale battery energy storage systems (BESS), standalone battery projects, battery procurement programs, flywheel storage, compressed air energy storage, thermal energy storage (standalone).

**Classification signals:** "battery storage", "BESS", "energy storage system", "MWh" (storage capacity), "battery procurement", "storage facility".

**Lesson from review:** Two projects were misclassified as Solar that were clearly Battery & Storage:
- "Uganda Co-located Battery Energy Storage System (250 MWh)" — "Battery Energy Storage System" with MWh capacity
- "South Africa Battery Storage Procurement Round" — "Battery Storage Procurement"

**CRITICAL distinction from Solar:** If a project is "100 MW Solar + 50 MWh Battery", classify as Solar (the battery is ancillary to the solar farm). But if the project is "250 MWh Battery Energy Storage System" that happens to be co-located with solar, classify as Battery & Storage (storage is the primary asset).

#### 8. Hydrogen
**Includes:** Green hydrogen production plants, electrolyzers, hydrogen pipelines, hydrogen refueling stations, ammonia-from-hydrogen plants, power-to-X facilities.

**Classification signals:** "hydrogen", "electrolyzer", "electrolysis", "green hydrogen", "H₂", "power-to-X", "green ammonia".

#### 9. Nuclear
**Includes:** Nuclear power plants, SMRs (small modular reactors), nuclear fuel facilities.

**Classification signals:** "nuclear", "SMR", "small modular reactor", "atomic energy", "nuclear power plant".

#### 10. Bioenergy
**Includes:** Biomass power plants, biogas digesters, waste-to-energy facilities, biofuel production plants, bagasse cogeneration, wood pellet plants.

**Classification signals:** "biomass", "biogas", "waste-to-energy", "biofuel", "bagasse", "cogeneration", "anaerobic digestion", "wood pellet".

#### 11. Clean Cooking
**Includes:** Improved cookstove programs, LPG cookstove distribution programs (when framed as clean cooking access, not fuel distribution), ethanol cookstove programs, electric cooking programs, biogas cooking systems.

**Classification signals:** "clean cooking", "cookstove", "improved stove", "cooking fuel", "electric cooking", "e-cooking".

#### 12. Coal
**Includes:** Coal-fired power plants, coal mine-mouth power stations, coal gasification plants.

**Classification signals:** "coal", "coal-fired", "coal plant", "coal gasification".

## PART 2: NON-ENERGY REJECTION RULES

The March 2026 review found that **90 of 180 projects** (50%) tagged as Solar were not energy projects at all. They were junk entries that the scraper should have rejected. Here are the patterns to reject:

### Category A: Electric Vehicles & Transport (NOT energy infrastructure)
**Reject if:** Project is an EV company, ride-hailing platform, electric motorcycle company, or transport logistics firm. These are NOT energy generation, storage, or grid infrastructure.

**Examples found in review (all were wrongly tagged Solar):**
- Electric motorcycle companies (Spiro, Roam, Ampersand)
- EV bus companies (BasiGo, Kayoola)
- Ride-hailing platforms operating EVs
- EV charging network companies (unless the project is specifically a charging station build — which would still not be Solar but could be Grid Expansion)

**Exception:** A dedicated EV charging infrastructure construction project (building physical charging stations powered by solar) could be classified. But the company itself or a fundraising round is NOT a project.

### Category B: World Bank / DFI Generic Development Programs
**Reject if:** The project is a World Bank IDA credit, IMF loan, or DFI program for generic development — governance, public sector reform, financial sector strengthening, social safety nets, digital transformation, agriculture, education, health.

**Signals to reject:**
- "IDA Credit" or "IDA Grant" for non-energy purposes
- "Public Financial Management"
- "Social Safety Net"
- "Digital Transformation"
- "Skills for" or "Skills Training"
- "Governance" or "Institutional Strengthening"
- "Health System Strengthening"
- "Agriculture" or "Agribusiness" or "Livestock"
- "Education" or "School" or "University"
- "Water Supply" or "Sanitation" or "WASH"
- "Road" or "Highway" or "Transport corridor" (unless it's an EV charging corridor)
- "Urban Development" or "Housing"
- "Financial Inclusion" or "Microfinance"

**The test:** Does the project description mention building, financing, or operating a PHYSICAL ENERGY ASSET (power plant, grid line, storage facility, etc.)? If not, reject it.

### Category C: Generic Corporate Fundraising
**Reject if:** The article is about a company raising venture capital, Series A/B/C funding, or debt financing, but there is no specific physical project being built.

**Examples:** "Solar startup raises $10M Series A" — this is a corporate event, not an energy project. REJECT unless the article also names a specific project the money will fund (e.g., "Solar startup raises $10M to build 50 MW plant in Kenya" — then extract the 50 MW plant, not the fundraising).

### Category D: Policy & Market Commentary
**Reject if:** The article is about energy policy, tariff changes, market analysis, forecasts, or regulatory developments with no named physical project.

**Examples:** "Nigeria raises electricity tariff", "Africa needs $100B in energy investment", "Morocco announces renewable energy target" — these are policy/commentary, not projects. REJECT.

**Exception:** "Morocco launches 2 GW solar tender" — this is a procurement action tied to specific capacity and could be extracted as a project. Use judgment.

### Category E: Conferences, Awards, Rankings
**Reject entirely.** "Africa Energy Forum", "Solar Industry Awards", "Top 10 Clean Energy Companies" — these are not projects.

## PART 4: AI EXTRACTION SYSTEM PROMPT (Updated)

Replace the existing scraper AI system prompt with this version:

```
You are an expert analyst specialising in African energy infrastructure investment and project finance. Your job is to extract ONLY genuine energy/power infrastructure projects from news articles.

WHAT TO EXTRACT:
- Physical energy infrastructure: power plants, solar farms, wind parks, hydro dams, geothermal wells, gas pipelines, LNG terminals, refineries, battery storage facilities, transmission lines, hydrogen plants, clean cooking facilities, nuclear plants, substations, interconnectors
- Investment/financing deals for the above (loans, equity, grants, PPAs, concessions) — but ONLY when a specific physical project is named
- Government procurement tenders for specific energy capacity
- DFI project approvals specifically for energy infrastructure

WHAT TO REJECT (return empty array):
- General development programs (World Bank/IDA social programs, health, education, agriculture, water/sanitation, governance, financial sector reform)
- EV companies, electric motorcycle startups, ride-hailing platforms (these are transport, not energy infrastructure)
- Corporate fundraising announcements that don't name a specific project
- Policy announcements without a named, physical project
- Climate finance pledges without a specific project
- Energy price news, fuel subsidy changes, tariff adjustments
- Opinion pieces, market commentary, conference announcements, awards
- Agriculture, fisheries, livestock, textiles, tourism, housing programs
- Digital transformation, skills training, education programs

FIELD RULES:
- projectName: The specific, unique project name. NEVER generic ("Energy Program", "Development Project"). Must name the physical asset or specific facility. If the article only discusses a company or market trend without naming a project, return [].
- country: A single African country name. NEVER a region. NEVER non-African. If the project spans multiple countries, use the primary host country.
- technology: MUST be exactly one of: "Solar", "Wind", "Hydro", "Geothermal", "Oil & Gas", "Grid Expansion", "Battery & Storage", "Hydrogen", "Nuclear", "Bioenergy", "Clean Cooking", "Coal"

  CLASSIFICATION RULES:
  - "Solar" = solar PV, CSP, solar thermal, solar mini-grids, solar home systems, solar-battery hybrids where solar is primary
  - "Wind" = onshore/offshore wind farms
  - "Hydro" = dams, run-of-river, pumped storage hydro, tidal, wave
  - "Geothermal" = geothermal power, steam fields, geothermal wells
  - "Oil & Gas" = upstream, midstream, downstream, refining, LNG, pipelines, gas-to-power, gas processing, LPG distribution
  - "Grid Expansion" = transmission lines, interconnectors, substations, distribution networks, rural electrification grid extensions
  - "Battery & Storage" = standalone BESS, battery procurement, grid-scale storage. Use this ONLY when storage is the PRIMARY asset, not an add-on to a solar/wind farm
  - "Hydrogen" = green hydrogen, electrolyzers, hydrogen pipelines, power-to-X, green ammonia
  - "Nuclear" = nuclear plants, SMRs
  - "Bioenergy" = biomass, biogas, waste-to-energy, biofuels, bagasse cogeneration
  - "Clean Cooking" = improved cookstoves, clean cooking fuel programs, e-cooking
  - "Coal" = coal-fired plants, coal gasification

  If you cannot clearly identify the technology, return [] (do NOT guess or default to Solar).

- dealSizeUsdMn: Value IN MILLIONS of US dollars.
  - "$500 million" = 500
  - "$1.2 billion" = 1200
  - "$50,000" = 0.05
  - NEVER above 5000. If it's a multi-country program budget (like a World Bank $2B portfolio), return null.

- status: One of "Announced", "Under Construction", "Operational", "Financing", "Tendered", "Cancelled", "Suspended". Default to "Announced" if unclear.

- capacityMw: In megawatts. Convert from GW (multiply by 1000) or kW (divide by 1000). For storage projects, this is power capacity (MW), not energy capacity (MWh).

- sourceUrl: The EXACT URL of the article you are extracting from. NEVER fabricate. NEVER return a homepage. If unknown, return null.

- confidenceScore: Your confidence this is a genuine African energy infrastructure project (0.0 to 1.0). Score below 0.5 if:
  - The project might be non-African
  - The "project" is actually a corporate event or policy announcement
  - You're unsure about the technology classification
  - The article is vague about what's actually being built

Return a JSON array. If nothing qualifies, return [].
```

## PART 5: POST-EXTRACTION VALIDATION CHECKLIST

Run these checks in code AFTER the AI returns its extraction, BEFORE inserting into the database:

1. **Technology must be in the canonical 12-sector list.** If not, reject.
2. **Country must be a valid African country** (54 AU member states). If not, reject.
3. **Project name must not match non-energy keywords:** Run the name through the exclusion list: "EV", "electric vehicle", "motorcycle", "ride-hailing", "governance", "social protection", "education", "health system", "agriculture", "livestock", "fisheries", "textile", "tourism", "digital transformation", "skills training", "financial inclusion", "microfinance", "water supply", "sanitation", "road construction", "housing". If ANY match, flag for human review.
4. **Deal size sanity:** If > $5,000M, set to null and flag for review. If < $0.05M, flag for review.
5. **Confidence threshold:** If confidenceScore < 0.6, route to review queue (do NOT auto-insert).
6. **Duplicate check:** Check for existing projects with similar names (fuzzy match, not just exact). Use Levenshtein distance or trigram similarity with threshold of 0.7.
7. **URL verification:** Confirm sourceUrl returns HTTP 200. If not, flag for review but still insert with null URL.

## PART 6: LESSONS LEARNED FROM THE MARCH 2026 REVIEW

### What Went Wrong
The original scraper had a default-to-Solar behavior: if the AI couldn't confidently classify a project, it was tagged "Solar". This caused 50% of the 180 Solar-tagged projects to be non-energy junk (World Bank governance loans, EV startups, agriculture programs, etc.).

### Key Fixes Applied
1. Split "Grid & Storage" into "Grid Expansion" and "Battery & Storage" — these are fundamentally different sectors
2. Added "Hydrogen" as a standalone sector (was buried in "Grid & Storage")
3. Reclassified 8 projects to their correct sectors (2 Battery, 2 Hydro, 4 Grid)
4. Rejected 90 non-energy projects that should never have been ingested
5. Confirmed 82 projects as legitimately Solar

### Rules to Prevent Recurrence
1. **NEVER default to Solar.** If the AI can't classify, return null and flag for review.
2. **Reject non-energy at the gate.** The keyword exclusion filter must run BEFORE AI extraction, not after.
3. **The AI prompt must explicitly list rejection categories** (EV, DFI generic programs, corporate fundraising, policy).
4. **Every new sector addition requires updating:** the normalization function, the AI prompt, the validation checklist, and the frontend config.
