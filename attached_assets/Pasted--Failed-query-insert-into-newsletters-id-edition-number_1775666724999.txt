Total tracked solar investment stands at **$43.7B**. However, the solar universe encompasses an unusually wide range of project types — from utility-scale CSP (Concentrated Solar Power) complexes in Morocco worth billions, to sub-$1M off-grid mini-grid deployments in rural Nigeria — making aggregate analysis less revealing than a disaggregated view. **Data limitation:** A material number of solar projects in the tracker have no disclosed financial size or capacity figure. The analysis below focuses on those with available data. ### North Africa: Mega-Scale, Multilateral-Backed North Africa — specifically **Egypt** and **Morocco** — hosts the tracker's largest individual solar investments by disclosed deal size. **Morocco's Noor complex** remains the anchor of the continent's solar story. The **Ouarzazate Solar Complex (NOOR)**, commissioned in 2012, represents **$2.4B in tracked investment** and **580 MW of capacity**, backed by the World Bank, AfDB, EIB, KfW, and the Climate Investment Funds (CIF). A separate but related entry — the **Ouarzazate Noor Midelt** project — records **$2.3B and 800 MW**, supported by EDF, Masdar, and Green of Africa with a project year of 2019. Together, the Ouarzazate corridor accounts for over **$4.7B in tracked solar investment** in Morocco alone, making it the single most capital-dense solar geography in the database. The **TuNur Solar Export Project** in Tunisia adds another notable entry: **$1.6B** in tracked investment for a project targeting **4,500 MW** of capacity — by far the largest capacity figure in the solar dataset. TuNur, backed by Mainstream Renewable Power and the EIB, is designed as a solar export project supplying power to European markets. No commissioning date or current stage is recorded in the tracker. In **Egypt**, the **Benban Solar Park** — a 1,650 MW facility backed by Scatec, JICA, IFC, and EBRD — was the flagship, recording **$2.0B** at entry in 2017. A 2022 **Benban Solar Park Expansion** entry records **$1.2B** from IFC, EBRD, and the European Investment Bank. The **Kom Ombo Solar PV Plant** adds **$182M** for 200 MW, backed by ACWA Power and IFC. Most significantly for the current reporting period, **Eni's announcement of a 2 tcf gas discovery offshore Egypt** — reported by Energy Capital Power on April 8 — reinforces Egypt's complex energy duality: advancing both large-scale solar and new hydrocarbon development simultaneously. This context is critical for understanding how solar investment competes with — and is sometimes crowded out by — gas sector capital allocation in North Africa. ### Southern Africa: REIPPPP as Institutional Architecture **South Africa** has the most mature solar regulatory framework on the continent, evidenced by the tracker's concentration of Renewable Energy Independent Power Producer Procurement Programme (REIPPPP) entries.
