## WHY THIS MATTERS

The PRs were shipped out of dependency order. The risk isn't that any single PR is wrong — it's that PR2 was built against a prerequisite (reviewer magic-link auth) that never existed, which means one of two things happened: (a) PR2 invented its own parallel auth system that now duplicates a piece of the codebase we'll have to ship later anyway, or (b) PR2 is half-done and broken in ways that aren't obvious yet. Either outcome is recoverable, but only if I can see the actual state of the tree. A rigorous audit with file/line citations is the only way to make that decision without guessing.
